FAQ's

Q: Why is the Pacific Ocean important?

A: The Pacific Ocean occupies half of the Earth's sea surface and more than one third of Earth's surface. Pollution and overfishing among other things threatens the delicate ecosystems and life found there.

Q: Where does most ocean pollution come from?

A: Most pollution that ends up in the ocean come from the land and is due to things such as runoff and garbage or plastic.

Q: How much plastic is in the Pacific Ocean?

A: During the past 40 years, plastic in the "Great Pacific Garbage Patch has increased 100-fold. This area of garbage is roughly the size of Texas.

Q: How does plastic impact marine life?

A: Jellyfish, seagulls, and other marine life get tangled in the plastic while Sea Turtles mistake plastic bags for Jellyfish, their favorite food.

Q: What area of the ocean is suffering the most from habitat destruction?

A: Costal areas are suffering the most from habitat destruction, which are the closest to the human population. Reasons for the loss of habitat are overfishing, pollution, mining, and poor costal planning.

Q: How can we create a cleaner ocean for the future?

A: To make a cleaner ocean for the future, changes can start at your home: carry your own reusable bags and reusable water bottle to cut back on the plastic you use.

-NBC News and National Geographic provided information for the answers to these questions-
